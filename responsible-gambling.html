3. Identifying Problem Gambling

Gambling should be an entertaining pastime, not a source of stress or financial difficulty. Here are some signs that gambling may be becoming a problem:

  • Spending more money and time on gambling than you can afford.
  • Chasing losses (trying to win back money you've lost).
  • Neglecting responsibilities (work, family, social life) due to gambling.
  • Lying about your gambling activities.
  • Feeling anxious, stressed, or irritable when you can't gamble.
  • Borrowing money or selling possessions to gamble.
  • Gambling affecting your relationships with others.
  • Difficulty stopping or controlling your gambling.

If you recognize any of these signs in yourself or someone you know, it's important to seek help.
